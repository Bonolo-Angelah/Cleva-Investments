2. DOMAIN ANALYSIS

2.1 General Field of Business

Cleva Investment operates in the Financial Technology (FinTech) domain, specifically in the Investment Advisory and Wealth Management sector. The platform provides AI-powered personalized investment recommendations to individual investors seeking to build wealth through strategic portfolio management.

2.2 Terminology and Glossary

Term Definition
Portfolio A collection of financial investments (stocks, bonds, ETFs) held by an investor
Risk Tolerance An investor's ability to withstand market volatility (conservative, moderate, aggressive)
Financial Goal A specific monetary target with a timeline (e.g., retirement, house purchase)
Market Data Real-time or historical information about stock prices, volumes, and trends
Investment Experience Investor's level of expertise (beginner, intermediate, advanced)
AI Chatbot Conversational interface powered by artificial intelligence for investment guidance
Graph Database Neo4j database storing relationships between users and investment preferences
Collaborative Filtering Recommendation technique based on similar users' investment behaviors
Holdings Specific assets owned within a portfolio
Transaction Buy, sell, or dividend action in an investment account

2.3 Business Environment Understanding

The investment advisory industry has traditionally been dominated by:

  • Human financial advisors charging 1-2% annual fees
  • Robo-advisors providing algorithmic portfolio management
  • Self-directed trading platforms requiring significant expertise
  • Limited accessibility for small investors (minimum investment requirements)

Current Market Trends:

  • Democratization of investing through mobile apps
  • AI-powered personalized recommendations
  • Goal-based investing approach
  • Real-time market data accessibility
  • Multi-asset portfolio diversification

2.4 Tasks and Procedures Currently Performed

Traditional investment advisory involves:

  1. Initial Consultation: Advisor meets client to understand goals and risk tolerance
  2. Portfolio Construction: Manual selection of investments based on client profile
  3. Ongoing Monitoring: Periodic reviews of portfolio performance
  4. Rebalancing: Adjusting asset allocation to maintain target risk levels
  5. Reporting: Quarterly or annual performance reports to clients
  6. Market Research: Advisors analyze market trends and news

2.5 Customers and Users

Target Users:

  • Beginner Investors: Individuals new to investing seeking guidance
  • Intermediate Investors: Those with some experience wanting AI-powered insights
  • Goal-Oriented Savers: People saving for specific life events (education, retirement, home)
  • South African Market: Primary focus on ZAR (South African Rand) currency users
  • Age Range: 25-55 years old, digitally literate individuals

User Demographics:

  • Middle-income earners seeking wealth building
  • Tech-savvy individuals comfortable with digital platforms
  • Users seeking lower-cost alternatives to traditional advisors

3. DEFINE THE PROBLEM

3.1 Problem Statement

Difficulty to Solve:

Many individual investors in South Africa and emerging markets face significant barriers to effective wealth building:

  1. High Advisory Costs: Traditional financial advisors charge 1-2% annual fees, making professional advice unaffordable for small investors
  2. Information Overload: Thousands of investment options overwhelm beginners
  3. Lack of Personalization: Generic advice doesn't account for individual goals, risk tolerance, and timelines
  4. Limited Accessibility: Geographic and time constraints prevent access to in-person advisors
  5. Knowledge Gap: Complex financial jargon and concepts intimidate new investors
  6. Delayed Decisions: Fear of making wrong choices leads to analysis paralysis and missed opportunities
  7. Currency Limitations: Most platforms focus on USD, not local currencies like ZAR

3.2 Opportunity

By leveraging AI and multi-database architecture, Cleva Investment can:

  • Democratize Investment Advice: Provide professional-grade recommendations to everyone
  • Reduce Costs: Eliminate high advisor fees through automation
  • Improve Productivity: Enable users to make informed decisions in minutes, not weeks
  • Increase Financial Literacy: Interactive chatbot educates while recommending
  • Drive Sales/Adoption: Attract users seeking modern, accessible investment solutions
  • Localized Solutions: Support South African Rand and local market conditions

Expected Benefits:

  • Users save 1-2% annually in advisory fees
  • Faster decision-making (3 minutes vs. 3 weeks for traditional advisory)
  • Increased investment participation among younger demographics
  • Better goal achievement rates through personalized tracking

  • Update: Modify profile information (name, contact, risk tolerance, experience)
  • Delete: Deactivate or permanently delete user account

7.2 Financial Goals

  • Create: Define new financial goal with target amount, timeline, and type
  • Read: View all goals, individual goal details, and progress tracking
  • Update: Modify goal parameters (target amount, date, priority, status)
  • Delete: Remove goals that are no longer relevant

7.3 Portfolios

  • Create: Establish new investment portfolio with name and description
  • Read: View portfolio details, holdings, performance metrics, and history
  • Update: Modify portfolio information, add/remove holdings, update values
  • Delete: Remove portfolio and associated holdings

7.4 Transactions

  • Create: Record buy, sell, or dividend transactions
  • Read: View transaction history with filters (date, type, symbol)
  • Update: Correct transaction details (quantity, price, date)
  • Delete: Remove erroneous transactions

7.5 Chat Sessions

  • Create: Initiate new conversation session with AI chatbot
  • Read: View chat history, previous conversations, and AI responses
  • Update: Continue existing conversations, add new messages
  • Delete: Clear chat history or end active sessions

Portfolio Calculations:
Total Value = Σ(Quantity × Current Price)
Total Cost = Σ(Quantity × Average Cost)
Total Gain/Loss = Total Value - Total Cost
Gain/Loss % = (Gain/Loss / Total Cost) × 100

8.2 Key Computations

1. Goal Progress Calculation:
Progress (%) = (Current Amount / Target Amount) × 100
2. Portfolio Gain/Loss:
Total Value = Σ(Quantity × Current Price)
Total Cost = Σ(Quantity × Average Cost)
Gain/Loss = Total Value - Total Cost
ROI (%) = (Gain/Loss / Total Cost) × 100
3. Average Cost Per Share:
Average Cost = Total Cost / Total Quantity
4. Time to Goal:
Months Remaining = (Target Date - Current Date) / 30 days
Required Monthly Savings = (Target Amount - Current Amount) / Months Remaining

10.4 Use Case 3: Add Transaction to Portfolio

Use Case Element Details
Use Case ID UC-003
Use Case Name Add Transaction to Portfolio
Primary Actor Investor
Goal User records an investment transaction (buy/sell) to track portfolio performance
Preconditions • User is registered and authenticated
• User has created at least one portfolio

Main Flow:

  1. User navigates to "Portfolios" section from dashboard
  2. System retrieves all user portfolios from PostgreSQL
  3. System displays portfolio list with summary cards
  4. User selects a portfolio to view details
  5. System displays portfolio detail page showing holdings, performance chart, and "Add Transaction" button
  6. User clicks "Add Transaction" button
  7. System displays transaction form
  8. User starts typing stock symbol (e.g., "AAPL")
  9. System calls GET /api/market/search?q=AAPL
  10. System displays autocomplete dropdown with matching stocks
  11. User selects stock from dropdown (e.g., "AAPL - Apple Inc.")
  12. User fills in quantity: 10 shares
  13. User fills in price: $150.00
  14. User selects transaction date
  15. User clicks "Add Transaction" button
  16. System validates input data
  17. System calculates total amount: Quantity × Price + Fees
  18. System saves transaction to PostgreSQL Transactions table
  19. System updates or creates holding in PortfolioHoldings table
  20. System fetches current price for symbol from Financial Modeling Prep API
  21. System calculates current value and gain/loss
  22. System updates portfolio totals in Portfolios table
  23. System redirects to portfolio detail page
  24. System displays success message: "Transaction added successfully!"
  25. System shows updated holdings with new values and gain/loss

Postconditions:

  • Transaction is recorded in database
  • Portfolio holdings are updated
  • Portfolio total value and gain/loss are recalculated
  • User can view updated portfolio performance

Business Rules:

  • Quantity must be positive integer for Buy/Sell transactions
  • Price must be positive number
  • Transaction date cannot be in the future
  • Average cost calculation for buys: ((Old Qty × Old Avg Cost) + (New Qty × New Price)) / Total Qty
  • Selling reduces quantity; if quantity reaches 0, holding is marked inactive but not deleted (for history)

11.8 Technology Selection Rationale

  1. React + Vite: Modern frontend stack with fast development experience, hot module replacement
  2. Express.js: Lightweight, flexible backend framework with middleware ecosystem
  3. Multi-Database: Each database serves specific purpose (relational, document, graph)
  4. Socket.IO: Enables real-time chat without complex WebSocket management
  5. Docker: Ensures consistent environments, simplifies deployment, enables easy scaling
  6. JWT: Stateless authentication suitable for RESTful APIs and mobile apps (future)
  7. Cohere AI: Free tier sufficient for MVP, good documentation, cost-effective
  8. Financial Modeling Prep: Reliable market data, generous free tier, comprehensive API
